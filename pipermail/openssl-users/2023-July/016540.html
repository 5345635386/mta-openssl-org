On Fri, Jul 21, 2023 at 01:59:40PM -0400, Robert Moskowitz wrote:

> I looked at a couple of certs.  I might think that if the first hex is 
> "F" then the 1st bit is 1, but:
> 
>      8:d=2  hl=2 l=   3 cons:   cont [ 0 ]
>     10:d=3  hl=2 l=   1 prim:    INTEGER           :02
>     13:d=2  hl=2 l=   9 prim:   INTEGER           :FE0E6F3753087370

OpenSSL `asn1parse` isn't showing you the leading `00` that keeps the
number positive.  Note that the encoded length is 9 bytes, but the
payload you see is only 8 bytes (16 nibbles):

    $ printf FE0E6F3753087370 | wc -c
      16

>      8:d=2  hl=2 l=   3 cons:   cont [ 0 ]
>     10:d=3  hl=2 l=   1 prim:    INTEGER           :02
>     13:d=2  hl=2 l=   9 prim:   INTEGER           :AEB77AEE2A3CBCD3

Ditto:

    $ printf AEB77AEE2A3CBCD3 | wc -c
      16
